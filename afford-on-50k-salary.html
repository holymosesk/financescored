$50,000 salary: how much house can you afford?

Short answer: somewhere between $190,000 and $320,000. Here's the math for all three tiers, and why 20% down isn't the only path in.

Your Range on $50,000

$190,000 – $320,000

Assumes 6.5% 30-yr fixed rate, 20% down. Don't have 20%? See the down payment breakdown below.

$50,000 is close to the median individual income in the US, which means this range answers the question a lot of people are actually asking, not a hypothetical one. Here's exactly where that income lands you, across all three lending tiers.

Mistakes that quietly cost $50K earners the most

  • Not accounting for other debt. The $295,000 Typical figure assumes minimal other monthly debt. A $300/month student loan payment can pull that down by $35,000-$45,000.
  • Skipping the credit score conversation. On a smaller loan amount, a rate difference matters just as much proportionally — a half-point swing still moves your buying power by tens of thousands.
  • Assuming you need to wait for 20% down. As the table above shows, waiting for 20% often costs more in rent paid while saving than it saves in PMI.
  • Ignoring HOA fees. On a tighter budget, a $100/month HOA has an outsized effect — it functions exactly like a higher mortgage payment.

Frequently asked questions

Can I afford a $250,000 house on a $50,000 salary?

It's within reach at the Typical tier (~$295,000) and comfortably inside the Maximum tier (~$320,000), assuming minimal other debt. It's above the Conservative estimate ($190,000), so it depends on how much breathing room you want to keep.

How much is the monthly payment on a $295,000 house?

With 20% down at 6.5%, expect roughly $1,500/month in principal and interest, plus about $334/month for taxes and insurance — about $1,833/month total.

Do I need 20% down on a $50,000 salary?

No. At the Typical tier, 5% down conventional or 3.5% down FHA both land around $235,000 in home price — close to the $295,000 possible with 20% down — while needing roughly $47,000-$51,000 less cash upfront.
