Why this exists

Most people graduate knowing how to solve for X, but not how credit scores work, how compound interest affects debt, or how to evaluate a mortgage. Those decisions can impact your finances for decades.

FinanceScored was built to help close that gap.

I'm Grant. I learned many of these lessons through experience, mistakes, and years of working inside financial institutions. Along the way, I earned a Bachelor of Science in Finance from Western Washington University and built a career spanning financial operations, compliance, and mortgage lending.

BS, Finance — Western Washington University 15+ years in financial operations & compliance Mortgage lending experience

I've seen financial decisions from both sides of the desk — as a consumer making mistakes and as a professional working inside financial institutions.

FinanceScored focuses on practical financial literacy, including credit, debt, budgeting, saving, investing, retirement planning, and major financial decisions. The goal isn't to make anyone a financial expert. It's to help people make educated decisions, avoid common mistakes, and build confidence with money.

How it works

Every calculator on this site uses standard financial formulas — the same ones lenders and financial planners use as a starting point. Type in your numbers, get a real answer in seconds, no signup required.

If you want a copy of your results emailed to you, that's optional — we only use that email to send the one report you asked for.

What this isn't

FinanceScored gives you a starting point, not personalized financial advice. I'm not a licensed financial advisor, and nothing here is investment, tax, or legal guidance — every result is an estimate based on the numbers you enter. Always confirm specifics with a licensed financial professional before making a major decision — a mortgage, a major investment, or a debt payoff plan that affects your credit.
